ABOUT US

FAQ

What is the best age to start playing chess?

As per my experience children start learning the basics around 4 years of age. But usually 5-6 is the age when they started to show interest and maybe get started with professional training

How important is the role of a parent?

I would like to suggest as a parent you can ⦁ Play with him whenever he likes to
⦁ Suggest buying books that might improve his chess
⦁ Teach them the importance of individual practice at home
⦁ Prevent your child from racing towards victory through shortcuts and instead encourage him/her to earn it through hard work and commitment
⦁ Plan everything to balance the time between academics, training and tournaments
⦁ Help your child handle expected and unexpected results in a balanced way. Chess is a sport, so winning and losing is completely normal

How important it is for the child to practice regularly?

⦁ Chess being an individual sport, a player should learn to think for himself/herself and take tough decisions all by himself/herself
⦁ It takes roughly 20 hours per week of practice to become a successful professional in any discipline. It would be ideal if a child, aspiring to become a Grand Master, can practice chess 2 hours a day
⦁ Half an hour or an hour devoted daily to the practice of the game will reap rich dividends over time both in terms of development of the brain and competitive results
⦁ Initial age of child(Age 4-5) Let the child enjoy the game and let them feel, touch whatever they want to do.

How important is it to get the right trainer for my child?

⦁ It is very essential to have a good trainer which motivate the child in a positive way
⦁ The trainer should incline your child more towards learning so that victories come automatically but not the other way round
⦁ The trainer must understand the potential of each and every child and every child is an exceptional individual with tremendous potential
